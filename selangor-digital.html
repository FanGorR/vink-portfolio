Overview

A first-person interactive virtual environment built in Unity, digitally recreating Museum Kota Kayang — a royal heritage landmark in Perlis, Malaysia documenting the state's history and cultural evolution.

Users explore the museum freely in first-person on PC, encounter artifacts room by room, and receive contextual voice narration and information pop-ups when approaching key exhibit zones.

The 4-month pipeline: full 3D modelling in Blender → import and assembly in Unity → interactive systems programming in C#.

Systems Built

3D Modelling (Blender)

  • Museum building, gazebo, benches, streetlamps from real-world reference
  • Progressive assembly in Unity across three documented stages

Unity Development (C#)

  • Automatic door triggers on proximity
  • Zone-based voice narration playback (weapons hall, history hall)
  • Artifact info pop-up panels with images and text
  • Morning natural light outdoors, spotlights for indoor display cases
  • Ambient background music and interaction sound effects
  • Main menu with Play, About, and Exit navigation
